Top 100 Song Predictor

The ML project that predicts what songs from 2014 made it to the Spotify Top 100

Ally Cody, Kevin Jin, Jaiveer Kothari, and Jeanette Pranin

EECS 349 ‐ Northwestern University

Introduction

Music has always had a profound influence on the way the world has shaped. It is often ordered and pleasant to listen to, and many modern cultures use music as a creative outlet to express themselves. Of course, some types of music and songs are more popular than others. There is no easy way of describing how popular a song could be when it is first created because its popularity is often due to how emotional or catchy the song is, but we were curious to see if we could use ML techniques to find any trend that could.

For this task, we used the Spotify and Echonest APIs in order to collect our data, which included the duration, explicitness, number of artists, danceability, energy, loudness, speechiness, and tempo of a track. We decided to use both Spotify and Echonest together because the two services partnered in recent years to create a seamless transition between their APIs, meaning we could use the Spotify IDs of all of the tracks to get information from Echonest.

We used Naive Bayes learners best accuracy for Top 100. Decision trees are terrible. Bayes Nets are okay. Nearest Neighbor are okay.
